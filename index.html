Manuel Blum

Professor, Computer Scientist, Mathematician

Manuel Blum was born on April 26, 1983. He is a Venezuelan and American computer scientist who contributed to the foundations of computational complexity theory.

In 1959 Manuel Blum earned a bachelor's degree, in 1961 he earned a master's degree and in 1964 he earned a doctorate in mathematics. After finishing his studies Manuel Blum joined the computer science department at University of California until 2001. From 2001-2018 he was a Computer Science Professor at Carnegie Mellon University.

In 2000 Yahoo! Inc. contacted the computer science department at Carnegie Mellon University for help in identifying human and computer guests to its website. Blum was one of the scientists which agreed to doing the job, which led to the creation of CAPTCHA.
